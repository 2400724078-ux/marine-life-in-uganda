About the Project

WELCOME TO OUR MARINE LIFE PROJECT

Uganda, known as the Pearl of Africa, is home to rich aquatic biodiversity within its lakes and rivers. From the great Nile perch to vibrant cichlids, these ecosystems support not only marine species but also the livelihoods of millions of Ugandans.

Our project documents, educates, and advocates for the preservation of these extraordinary underwater worlds.

Conservation

ENVIRONMENTAL CHALLENGES

Pollution, overfishing, and invasive species threaten the balance of Uganda's marine ecosystems. Conservation and awareness are crucial to protect these precious resources.

🏭
Pollution

Industrial and agricultural runoff is contaminating Uganda's lakes and rivers at alarming rates.

🎣
Overfishing

Unsustainable fishing practices are depleting fish populations faster than they can naturally recover.

🌿
Invasive Species

Non-native species like water hyacinth are disrupting natural ecosystems and blocking waterways.
